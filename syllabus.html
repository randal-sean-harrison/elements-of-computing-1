Course Goals
Description

As we move into the twenty-first century, we are experiencing a paradigm shift in the way we interpret information and make decisions in many fields — everything from business and medicine to social networking and dating. While gathering data to inform our decisions is an important skill, an often overlooked skill is the ability to communicate that data impactfully. In this class you'll learn techniquest to motivate audiences and stakeholders to adopt the insights that you, as the data analyst, offer them. This course will focus on the tools and processes to create data-rich deliverables crucial to communicating the insights that move your stakeholders toward greater understanding of the data, and greater acceptance of the action items you recommend. Students will leave the course having completed the deliverables at each stage of the process of developing data-rich visuals.

Objectives

In this course, you will learn to:

  1. Create impactful charts and graphs in spreadsheets
  2. Think ethically about data, to spot and avoid using misleading tactics in charts and graphs
  3. Master the fundamentals of Gestalt Theory to create stunning data-rich documents, presentation slides and 'slidedocs'
  4. Use the principles of 'data storytelling' to create impactful presentations
  5. Use Tableau to create a dyanmic data dashboard
  6. Learn to create a simple web page and upload it to GitHub

Due Dates & Late Work
Due Dates

All due dates are presented in Eastern Standard Time (EST). Students are responsible for any time zone conversion required when submitting assignments. If you're confused by the time-conversion, the current time in South Bend (EST) is presented in the banner at the top of this website.

Late Work

Late work will be excused only for documented medical emergencies. Unexcused late assignments will still receive credit, but will be adjusted downward by 10% of total assignment points for each day they are late.

Attendance & Participation
Attendance

Attendance is mandatory and essential for obtaining a passing grade in the class. Students are allowed 2 penalty-free absences. Absences will only be excused for medical emergencies and when university-required service conflicts. Absences beyond a second may affect your participation grade. If you know you will have to miss class, please contact your instructor beforehand.

Participation

Your participation grade is made up of your participation during class discussions and the percentage of resources you contribute to the Curating Resources assignment.

You needn't make some arbitrary number of comments per class, but I should have a sense at the end of the semester that you contributed positively to our class discussions. Participating in this way helps you to both contribute to and leverage the collective intelligence of the group. During class, you will be expected to have your camera turned on, though you may mute the camera briefly, as necessary. You may mute your mic, as well, but please be be ready to respond quickly when called on.

Grade Curving

Mendoza College of Business policy requires the undergraduate school grade point average range for a course must be between 3.0 - 3.6. It may be necessary to “curve” grades to comply with this policy.

Grade Review

Grading is a subjective and holistic process, and though I strive to make it as objective as possible, my role is to evaluate your work based on my best thinking as an expert in this field. If you believe there was an error and you'd like to challenge your grade, please first examine your assignment again with regard to both the prompt and any rubric provided. If you still feel the need for a grade review use the grade review request form to argue your case.
